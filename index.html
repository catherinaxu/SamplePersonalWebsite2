Wake Me Up

This app was created with Kristen Law at Treehacks 2015. We are currently working on adding more features.

Ever miss a train stop because you were snoozing? Ever refrained from napping on public transportation because you were anxious? Wake Me Up combines your phone + Pebble and makes sure that you wake up before your stop. The app uses the Google Play Services API and tracks your location over time through your Android device, updating every 5 seconds and displaying crucial travel data such as speed, destination, and geographical coordinates. When you are within a certain radius of your destination (currently supports various Caltrain stations), your Pebble will buzz on your wrist until you have woken up and silenced it.

Intended for anyone and everyone who wants to catch a good nap on a long train, car, or bus ride!

My Travel Diary

My Travel Diary is an Android applications that allows nostalgic users to record details about places they've visited throughout their lifetime. With Google Maps API, and SQLite persistent storage, users are able to add, update, and delete markers on a world map, as well as assign a short description to each location.

Support for image uploads, multi-colored markers, and search suggestions coming soon!

CampusBubble

This app was created with Rishab Mehra, Theo Diamandis, Jialin Ding, and Konner Robison at the Accenture Hackathon Games 2014.

Inspired by our inability to find gym buddies while on campus, we created this iPhone app to encourage the spontaneous formation of gym, dinner, and study-groups with the collegiate community. The app uses PubNub API to enable real-time updates of your friends' whereabouts, eliminating the need for the excessive messaging often required to plan meet-ups. CampusBubble also features a simple textbook exchange service in Bitcoin, facilitated through BitPay API - the app went on to win the BitPay award.

UberPrepared

This app was created with Kristen Law and Ryan Perry-Nguyen at Hack the Planet 2015.

Have you ever wanted to call an Uber but couldn't because there was no data service? Or maybe you wanted an Uber to pick you up after a movie and wished you could order the Uber ahead of time? Well now you can! This app uses Twilio Messaging, Uber, and Google Places API to bring you an Uber pre-scheduling interface through both SMS and Android. After setting the pickup location, destination, and arrival time using natural language commands, the backend will intelligently book an Uber at just the right time so you'll have an Uber waiting for you when you need it.
